SmartTube - YouTube for TV

SmartTube is an open-source, ad-free version of the YouTube app for Android TVs and TV boxes. It offers all the same features as the official YouTube app, plus a few additional features like the ability to block annotations and sponsor segments.

SmartTube Features

  • Ad-free experience: SmartTube blocks all ads, including pre-roll, mid-roll, and post-roll ads. This allows you to watch videos uninterrupted and without any distractions.
  • SponsorBlock integration: SmartTube integrates with SponsorBlock, which allows you to skip sponsored segments of videos. This is a great way to save time and avoid watching content that you are not interested in.
  • Adjustable playback speed: SmartTube allows you to watch videos at a variety of playback speeds, from 0.25x to 2.0x. This is a great way to speed up videos that you are not interested in or to slow down videos that are too fast.
  • 8K support: SmartTube supports 8K video playback, so you can watch your favorite videos in stunning detail.
  • 60fps support: SmartTube supports 60fps video playback, so you can enjoy smooth and fluid video playback.
  • HDR support: SmartTube supports HDR video playback, so you can experience videos with a wider range of colors and contrast.
  • Read live chat: SmartTube allows you to read live chat while you are watching a video. This is a great way to interact with other viewers and to stay up-to-date on the latest comments.
  • Customizable buttons: SmartTube allows you to customize the buttons in the video player. This is a great way to make the video player work the way you want it to.
  • No Google Services: SmartTube does not require Google Services, so you can use it even if you have not installed Google Play Services on your device.
  • Helpful international community: SmartTube has a large and helpful international community. If you have any questions or problems, you can always ask for help in the community forum.

Disadvantages: no support for phones and tablets, no comments stability, voice search and casting support might be worse than in the official apps, depending on your device; give it a try!
